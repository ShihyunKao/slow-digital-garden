# Trail V03: Path Constellation

## Question

How can slow hand movement be recorded as a constellation-like pathway rather than as a continuous drawing line?

## Change from V02

V02 tested ink and water-like material behaviour. Its visual language became too illustrative and noisy. V03 replaces this with a quieter system of nodes, links and pauses.

## Interaction

Move one index finger through the space. The system deposits points along the movement path and connects them into a personal constellation. Slower movement creates brighter, more substantial points. Holding still creates a larger anchor point.

`M` switches between Mouse and HandPose input. `P` cycles hand visibility between hidden, point and skeleton. `R` clears the path. `?` opens the help screen.

## Design intention

This is the **Path** study within the wider movement series:

- Open records a momentary hand state.
- Trail records a path through space.
- Both Hands records a complete stretching cycle.

The work does not draw directly under the hand. It translates movement into a sparse record that can be read afterwards as a bodily constellation.

## What to observe

- Does the path feel more coherent when the movement is slow?
- Does a pause create a meaningful anchor without becoming a task or game?
- Is the visual language distinct from the orbit-based Both Hands studies?

## Next development

Test whether steadiness, direction changes or repeated paths can subtly affect the structure of the constellation.
