# Trail V04: Slow Orbit Drawing

## Question

Can slowness become the condition that allows a scattered movement trace to form a coherent visual structure?

## Change from V03

V03 recorded movement as a path constellation. V04 focuses on movement quality. A new path begins as unstable points; sustained slow movement gradually draws those points together into a stable orbit-like line. Fast movement breaks the structure apart.

## Interaction

Move one index finger through the space. Slow, continuous movement increases coherence. When the path becomes stable, pause briefly to preserve it. Moving too quickly disperses the current path into particles.

`M` switches between Mouse and HandPose input. `P` cycles hand visibility between hidden, point and skeleton. `R` clears the drawing. `?` opens the help screen.

## Design intention

This experiment does not reward speed or accuracy. Visual coherence emerges through patience, steadiness and sustained attention. The final drawing becomes a record not only of where the hand moved, but of how it moved.

## Technical experiment

- Smoothed fingertip tracking
- Speed-sensitive coherence
- Scattered-to-stable point interpolation
- Automatic path preservation after a pause
- Particle dispersal during fast movement

## What to observe

- Is the difference between slow and fast movement immediately perceptible?
- Does the gradual appearance of the orbit encourage slower movement?
- Does pausing to preserve a path feel understandable?

## Next development

Test whether repeated slow paths can interact, overlap or gradually form a larger movement archive.
