# 03 Breathing Garden

## Question
Can the distance between two hands become a slow and calming upper-body movement exercise?

## Interaction
When both hands are close together, the visual field remains compressed. As the hands slowly move apart, layered contour lines expand across the screen.

## Design intention
The visual reference is not a literal flower garden. It draws instead from raked sand, contour maps, water ripples, and the spatial rhythm of a garden landscape.

## What to observe
- Does the hand movement feel physically clear and comfortable?
- Does the gradual expansion create a sense of breathing or release?
- Does the contour field feel visually calm rather than decorative?

## Next possible development
Connect the expansion to sound, such as a subtle low-frequency tone, soft wind, or sparse bell-like texture.

## Tutor-feedback refinement
This version was adjusted after feedback that the field remained too broad when hands were close together. The interaction now measures the distance between both index fingertips rather than both wrists. The number, width, and height of the contour lines also reduce together, so touching the fingertips creates a near-closed centre.
