# Open V05: Pressure Bloom

## Question

How can an opening palm be translated into a moment of release without illustrating the hand itself?

## Change from V04

V04 recorded a held palm as a constellation-like imprint. V05 removes the palm outline entirely. The hand becomes a pressure source: opening it creates a soft field at the palm position, while each completed opening leaves a circular memory that slowly dissipates.

## Interaction

Open one palm to create a live pressure field. Close the hand before opening again to leave another soft circular imprint.

`M` switches between Mouse and HandPose input. `P` cycles hand visibility between hidden, point and skeleton. `R` clears the field. `?` opens the help screen.

## Design intention

This is the **Moment** study in the movement series. It treats a single opening gesture as a quiet release: a temporary spatial disturbance rather than a flower, image or literal body outline.

## What to observe

- Does the field feel responsive without becoming decorative?
- Does the circular imprint suggest pressure, breath or release?
- Does the absence of a hand outline make the interaction feel less literal?

## Next development

Test whether the steadiness or duration of an opening can affect the density and duration of the pressure memory.
